What is DigiMyths?

As first-generation citizens, third-culture kids, or others with a familial attachment to another country, this other place often exists as an idea in our own heads. Complicated even further by COVID-19, the place begins to fade as frequent summer trips become sporadic, and maintaining one’s heritage becomes harder. What is Digital Folklore? While it’s definitions are amorphous, one way to see it is as a manner reconnecting with that heritage through understanding customs, stories, and beliefs without the need for geo-physicality. Join Kevin Cadena, BabyCastles’ Digital Folklorist-in-Residence for a DigiMyths: a program on the intersection of digital technologies, heritage, and familial stories.

The participants and instructor will convene on Zoom to start the program in early February, and to end the program in late February. During these two weeks, participants will research mythologies related to their homeland and/or culture, sketch out potential ideas melding computer technologies into these stories, produce drafts to actualize their concepts and create self-driven projects that explore their own interpretation of Digital Folklore. To further supplement their projects, I will host a series of co-working sessions on Wednesdays between the two weeks for those who need time set aside and gather resources and materials that showcase some ways that artists have recontextualized their heritage through digital media.

Participants should either be enrolled in a degree-granting arts program or have had to recently stop their studies due to COVID-19 related complications. Participants should also have a connection with another country outside of the one they are residing in—either through their parents, grandparents, etc—and are looking for new ways to engage with that homeland. Participants will work on self-directed projects over the course of two weeks culminating in a final presentation of the work done and where it will go.

Who is involved?

Kevin Cadena

Kevin is a graphic designer, web developer, and educator currently based in NYC. As an educator, he has taught at the Parsons Scholars Program a 3-year needs-based college-readiness program for NYC high school students working at the intersection of art and social justice along with teaching as an adjunct faculty member in the design department at the School of Visual Arts. He am currently a digital-folklorist-in-residence at Babycastles, an NYC collective fostering diverse voices in videogames.

Babycastles

Babycastles is a NYC based collective fostering and amplifying diverse voices in videogame culture. Babycastles provides artists support to actualize ideas and expose that work to new audiences.

Babycastles primarily exhibit videogames, but also feature visual art, digital media, and mixed media installations. Babycastles host a variety of events up to four nights a week, including exhibition openings and closings, artist talks, presentations, readings, live music and coding shows, game development workshops, game jams, creative salons, student showcases, coworking nights, and more.
